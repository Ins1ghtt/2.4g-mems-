Experiment: EXP-001 / v0.5-rf-throughput-optimization
Date: 2026-04-23

Goal:
- Push the private 2.4 GHz link toward 50 ksps x 16-bit = 800 kbps payload.
- Reduce the bottleneck observed in v0.4, where 1 Mbps ESB + ACK could not
  drain 32-sample frames every 640 us.

Firmware parameters:
- PHY: ESB 4 Mbps on nRF54L15 when available, otherwise 2 Mbps fallback.
- ACK mode: no-ACK streaming payloads.
- Channel: 40.
- Frame size: 204 bytes.
- Samples per frame: 96 x 16-bit.
- TX period: 1920 us.
- Effective target: 96 samples / 1.92 ms = 50 ksps, 800 kbps payload.
- HP TX queue depth: 64 frames.
- ESB TX/RX FIFO size: 16.
- UART status period: 1000 ms.

Code changes to verify:
- TX and RX proto.h agree on 96 samples and 204-byte wire frame.
- TX and RX radio_link.c agree on PHY, channel, CRC, and no-ACK behavior.
- TX FLPR main sends generated frames directly to IPC every 1920 us.
- TX HP main sends frames with a short 5 ms MAC timeout and time-based status.
- RX main reports one stats line per second.

Measurement procedure:
- Flash TX with build_rf_link_tx and RX with build_rf_link_rx.
- Open RX UART and TX UART at 115200 baud.
- Capture both streams with save_serial_csv.py for at least 60 seconds.
- Record board distance, antenna orientation, and room/environment.

Metrics to record:
- RX bps average and range.
- RX lost delta per second.
- RX bad and dup deltas.
- TX q_drop delta per second.
- TX rf_fail and rf_timeout deltas.
- TX mac_min_us, mac_avg_us, mac_max_us.

Pass criterion:
- RX bps should approach 800000 bit/s with much lower lost and q_drop than
  EXP-000.

Current result:
- Build verified locally.
- Hardware UART/CSV measurement is pending.
